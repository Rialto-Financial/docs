Byron's business tax tool is built around agents that automate the work across the lifecycle of a business tax return. From the moment a client is set up, Byron handles intake, document processing, indexing, trial balance generation, workpaper preparation, and export — surfacing each step for your team to review and sign off as it goes.

This section covers what Byron does at each stage of a business return and how the workflow fits together end to end.

## What Byron does for a business return

Once a client is created, the return moves through the following stages:

- **Intake.** Byron drafts an intake request asking the client for last year's tax return, work papers, last year's tax software file, and a questionnaire.
- **Document processing.** As documents arrive, Byron's agent processes each one live so your team can see what's happening as it happens.
- **Index.** Byron populates a full index of what's going to be in the return — entity information, tax year, filing scope, states, a preview of the chart of accounts, and the book-to-tax items.
- **Document manifest.** Byron's tax engine identifies the additional documents needed for the coming year based on what's been received so far, then drafts a follow-up email to the client requesting them.
- **Trial balance.** Byron generates the trial balance from the financials provided. You can edit it directly in the browser before signing off.
- **Workpapers.** Byron generates the first cut of the workpapers — partner capital accounts, tax account mappings, carry forwards, roll forwards. Team members can sign off in parallel.
- **Forms.** When workpapers are ready, Byron produces the filled-in tax return PDFs alongside per-form callouts your team can review.
- **Export.** The final return data exports to your tax software, and the workpapers export to Excel with two-way sync.

For the step-by-step walkthrough, see [How Byron works for business tax](/business-tax/how-byron-works).

## Audit log

Every action — taken by a Byron agent or by a member of your team — is captured in an audit log so the firm has a clear record of who did what and when.
