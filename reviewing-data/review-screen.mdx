From the client dashboard, click any document in **Ready for Review** status to open the review screen. This is where you confirm what the agent extracted. The screen is built around a single idea: you should never have to leave the page or switch windows to verify a field. The source document sits on the left and every field Byron pulled sits on the right.

This page walks through the layout so you know what each part of the screen is for.

<Info>
  For the actual review workflow — approving fields, leaving comments, using anchors, reading the audit log — see [Review process](/reviewing-data/review-process).
</Info>

<Accordion title="The PDF panel (left side)">
  The left half of the screen is a live view of the document Byron processed. The toolbar across the top of the panel gives you everything you need to inspect the source without opening the file elsewhere.

  From left to right, the toolbar controls are:

  - **Page navigation.** For multi-page documents (a K-1 packet, a brokerage 1099 consolidated statement), the page navigator lets you jump between pages.
  - **Hand / pan tool.** Click and drag to move around a zoomed-in page without changing zoom level. Useful when you're verifying a small number (a basis figure, an EIN) and want to stay zoomed in as you scan the page.
  - **Zoom in / zoom out.** Zoom up to read fine print — the scans Byron accepts can come in at any resolution, so a coffee-stained faxed 1099 often needs a closer look than a clean digital PDF.
  - **Fill / fit view.** Switch to fit the page view to zoom out on the file.
  - **Search.** Type any string to find it in the document text. Matches highlight in place.
  - **Rotate.** For documents that came in sideways or upside down after scanning.

  Most of the time you won't touch the toolbar. When Byron flags a field, clicking the field on the right panel auto-scrolls the PDF to the exact spot it came from and frames it in a highlight box. That's the primary way you'll navigate the source. The toolbar is there for the cases where you want to look around on your own.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="The Extracted Data panel (right side)">
  The right half of the screen is everything Byron extracted from the document, grouped by section.

  Two sets of controls sit at the top of this panel:

  <Tabs>
    <Tab title="1. Action buttons">
      The top-right of the panel shows **Save**, **Reject**, and **Approve**. Save holds your in-progress changes without closing the review. Reject sends the document out of the filing — use it when Byron misidentified the form type, when the document belongs to a different client, or when the scan is unreadable. Approve marks the document as reviewed and ready for export. Approving is the action that releases a document into the rest of the filing.

      Rejecting and approving are both reversible from the filing workspace until you export.
    </Tab>
    <Tab title="2. Filter tabs">
      ### Filter tabs

      Below the action buttons, a row of tabs lets you filter the field list by review status. The counts update as you work through the document:

      - **All** — every field Byron extracted.
      - **Filled** — all fields where Byron returned a value, regardless of confidence.
      - **High** — fields the confidence model placed in the high-confidence bucket. These are the ones you can usually skim past.
      - **Review** — fields Byron wants you to look at. A zero here means nothing is outstanding.
      - **Corrected** — fields you've edited during this review.

      A download icon at the far right of the tab row lets you download the document if you need the full file for your workpapers, a client email, or a second pair of eyes.
    </Tab>
  </Tabs>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="The anchor link between the two panels">
  Every field Byron extracted is linked to its source location on the PDF it came from. This can be switched on and off with the anchors toggle that sits at the top of the right panel.

  Click a field and the PDF panel scrolls and zooms to that spot, with a colored box around the source text. This is how you verify a value without reading the whole form — Byron tells you where it got the number, and you decide whether the number matches. Anchors are covered in detail in [Review process](/reviewing-data/review-process).
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Collapsed vs. expanded sections">
  Sections start collapsed or expanded based on Byron's confidence across the section as a whole.

  - **Collapsed by default.** When every field in a section landed in the high-confidence bucket and there are no open warnings, the section collapses and shows an **Acacia Validated** badge next to the section name and field count. The assumption is that you don't need to look at these — the agent is confident, and nothing is flagged. Click the section header to expand it if you want to verify anyway.
  - **Expanded by default.** Sections with any field in the review bucket, any warning, or any correction open up automatically. The agent is drawing your attention to where it wants a human pass.

  Status chips sit next to each field inside an expanded section. Common ones include:

  - `High Confidence` (a plain chip, no action needed)
  - `Validated` (you've already approved this field)
  - `Warning` (unresolved flags on this field)
  - `Corrected` (you edited this field from Byron's original extraction)

  See [Understanding confidence scores](/reviewing-data/confidence-scores) for further information on these status chips.
</Accordion>
