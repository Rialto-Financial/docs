---

Every field Byron extracts comes back with a confidence score. The score is Byron's assessment of how likely the extracted value is correct, and it drives what you see on the review screen — which sections collapse, which fields get a `High Confidence` chip, and which ones land in the Review queue.

This page explains what each level means in practice, and how the score should change the way you work through a document.

## How confidence is determined

Byron assigns confidence based on historical accuracy for each field path on each form type. The system tracks how often a specific field (for example, Box 1 on a W-2) has needed a correction across all documents processed. Fields that rarely need corrections land in the High bucket; fields that are corrected more often land in Review.

Under the hood, Byron uses three confidence bins — High, Medium, and Low. Medium and Low both surface as Review on the review screen, because both warrant a closer look. If a field has no historical data yet (a new form type or a field Byron hasn't seen enough of), it defaults to High.

## The filter tabs and what to do at each level

The filter tabs at the top of the right-hand panel let you slice the field list by confidence and edit status.

- **All** is every field Byron extracted.
- **Filled** is everything where Byron returned a value, regardless of confidence — High and Review combined, plus any field you've already touched. It's the catch-all view.
- **High** is the agent's "I'm confident, you can skim past this" bucket.
  <Tip>
    You can deprioritize fields with High confidence level in your review process. You can trust them on the first pass and return for further review at the final stage before approving the document.
  </Tip>

- **Review** is the queue Byron is asking you to look at closely. The Review tab is where most preparers start a review.
  <Tip>
    Click into the field, look at the anchored region on the PDF, and decide. Either the value Byron pulled matches the source — in which case approve the field — or it doesn't, in which case edit it. Either action clears it from the Review queue.
  </Tip>
- **Corrected** is fields that have been edited from Byron's original extraction. It's a sanity-check view: before approving, glance at Corrected to confirm you didn't introduce a typo while fixing something else.
  <Tip>
    Skim them once before approving the document. The audit log captures each edit with the original value, your new value, and who made the change.
    A field can move between states as you work — editing a Review field marks it Corrected, and approving a Review field marks it Validated.
  </Tip>

## How confidence shapes the screen

The confidence pattern across a document is what decides whether a section opens or stays collapsed when you land on the review screen. Sections where every field scored High and nothing is flagged start collapsed. Sections with any field in the Review bucket, any warning, or any correction open automatically so you see them first.

## Confidence and your firm's review policy

The confidence score is a signal, and you can decide how you want to review and approve fields with various levels of confidence. These confidence levels are here to facilitate your review process.
