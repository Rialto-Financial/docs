# How Byron works

Byron replaces manual data entry for tax document processing. Instead of keying in W-2s, 1099s, and K-1s by hand, you upload the source documents and Byron extracts the data for you — with 98–99% accuracy. You review what Byron found, make any corrections, and export directly into your tax preparation software.

The entire process follows four steps.

## Step 1: Upload your documents

Drop your client's tax documents into Byron. You can upload a single document, drag and drop an entire folder, or upload multiple files in bulk.

Byron currently processes **PDF files**. You can upload scanned documents, digital PDFs, and composite statements. There's no need to sort or label anything before uploading — Byron automatically identifies the document type (W-2, 1099-INT, K-1, etc.) and routes it to the right place.

<Note>
Byron accepts folders but not zip files. If your documents arrive as a zip, extract them first, then upload the folder.
</Note>

## Step 2: Byron extracts the data

Once uploaded, Byron's AI reads each document and extracts the relevant data fields. This typically takes under a minute per document. For a K-1 with footnotes and state schedules, it may take slightly longer.

Here's what Byron does during extraction:

- **Identifies the document type** — W-2, 1099, K-1, Schedule C, or brokerage statement
- **Reads every field** — from simple boxes like wages and interest income to complex breakouts like Section 199A by activity
- **Pulls from footnotes** — for K-1s, Byron doesn't just read the face sheet. It goes into the footnotes and supplemental schedules to extract detailed breakouts
- **Runs validation checks** — Byron flags potential issues like year mismatches, recipient name or TIN mismatches, and blank forms with no reportable amounts
- **Assigns confidence scores** — each field gets marked as either "high confidence" (likely correct) or "review" (deserves closer attention)

## Step 3: Review and approve

This is where you stay in control. Byron presents a **side-by-side view** — the original PDF on the left, the extracted data on the right.

A few things are designed to save you time:

- **Validated sections collapse automatically.** If Byron matched the recipient information and found no issues, that section is already collapsed so you don't waste time re-checking it. Sections with warnings stay open and draw your attention.
- **Anchors show you exactly where each number came from.** Click on any field and Byron highlights the spot on the PDF where it pulled the data. You can choose between "jump and zoom" (takes you right to it) or "scroll into view" (shows a pointer arrow).
- **Filter by status.** If you want to focus on the fields that need the most attention, filter by "review" to see only the fields Byron is less confident about. Once those are handled, switch to "filled" to check the rest.
- **Edit anything.** If Byron got a number wrong, just type the correct value. The field updates to "corrected" and logs who changed it and when.
- **Approve as you go.** Check off each field or use the bulk approve option to approve an entire document at once. Every approval is tracked in the audit log with timestamps and user info, so your team always knows who reviewed what.

<Tip>
For K-1s with many fields, a common approach is to filter by "review" first, approve those individually, then bulk approve the remaining high-confidence fields.
</Tip>

### Validation warnings you'll see

Byron automatically flags issues that need your attention. Here are the most common:

- **Year mismatch** — the document's tax year doesn't match the return year. This can happen legitimately with real estate taxes charged in one year and paid in the next.
- **Recipient mismatch** — the recipient name on the document doesn't match the taxpayer or spouse. This often comes up with trusts, disregarded entities, or 1099-Qs issued to a dependent child.
- **No reportable amounts** — the form exists but has no dollar figures on it. Common with 1099-MISC or 1099-OID pages in a composite statement that don't apply to your client.

In every case, **you have the final say.** You can override any automated decision — approve a flagged document, reject one that was auto-approved, or correct any field.

## Step 4: Export to your tax software

Once you've reviewed and approved everything, click **Create Export** in the top right corner. Byron supports the following export formats:

| Format | Status |
| --- | --- |
| Lacerte Tax | Available |
| CCH Axcess Tax | Available |
| UltraTax CS | Available |
| Drake Tax | Coming soon |
| GoSystem Tax | Coming soon |
| Excel workbook | Available |
| Combined PDF | Available |

For direct tax software imports, Byron formats the data to match your platform's import requirements — no reformatting or manual re-entry needed.

The **Excel work paper** export is especially useful for review. It includes:

- **Hyperlinked tabs** for every document type (W-2, 1099, K-1, Schedule C)
- **A K-1 summary tab** that consolidates all K-1 data by line across every K-1 in the client, with working formulas you can adjust (e.g., toggling between passive and non-passive)
- **A consolidated state K-1 summary** that rolls up all state-level K-1 data across every K-1, helping you quickly identify which states require filing

## What happens with prior year data

If your firm has been using Byron or has prior year data available, Byron can **roll forward** client information from the previous year. This means your client's expected documents are pre-populated — when you upload this year's W-2s and K-1s, Byron automatically matches them to the prior year entries.

For firms that are new to Byron or don't have roll forward data, you simply create a new client and upload documents fresh. Byron handles everything from there.

## Putting it all together

Here's what a typical workflow looks like during tax season:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Receive documents from your client">
    Client sends their W-2s, 1099s, K-1s, and other tax documents — however they normally do (email, portal, in person).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload to Byron">
    Drop the documents into the client's folder in Byron. Upload the whole batch at once.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Byron processes everything">
    Within minutes, every document is classified, extracted, and validated. You'll see them appear in the client's document list.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the extraction">
    Open each document, check the flagged fields, approve the rest. For most documents, this takes a fraction of the time manual entry would.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Export to your tax software">
    Create an export in your preferred format. Import it into Lacerte, CCH Axcess, UltraTax, or download as Excel work papers for further review.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Prepare the return">
    Continue your return preparation as usual — with the data already entered.
  </Step>
</Steps>
