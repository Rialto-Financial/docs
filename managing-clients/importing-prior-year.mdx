---
title: "Importing prior year data"
description: "Bring last year's return context into Byron so clients roll forward cleanly — set up once, reuse every season."
---

Byron works best when it knows what your firm already had in Lacerte or CCH Axcess before this tax year began. Prior year data — basis balances, naming conventions, election carryovers, rolled-forward client records — is what lets Byron match new documents to existing clients and keep your return in sync with your tax software.

You bring in prior year data once, at setup, and Byron reuses it every season after that. How you do it depends on which tax software your firm uses.

## Which import path applies

**CCH Axcess firms** do a one-time bulk export of every pro forma return for the current tax year, packaged as a `.dat` file. Byron ingests it, populates all your rolled-forward clients into the client list with a **Rolled Forward** status, and keeps the CCH client IDs attached so exports land on the right returns.

**Lacerte firms** use a file called iData, delivered per filing. There's no bulk import step — each filing you start brings its own iData at export time, which is what gives Byron the basis tracking and naming continuity for that client.

## CCH Axcess: one-time bulk import

Do this once at setup. You can import every pro forma 1040 in a single pass, or go client-by-client if you'd rather roll out Byron gradually.

<Note>
  Before you export, make sure the clients you want to bring over are already rolled forward to the current tax year in CCH. Byron populates existing rolled-forward CCH records — it doesn't create new ones.
</Note>

### Export from CCH

1. Open **Return Manager** and filter to this tax year's pro forma returns.
2. Select the returns you want to send to Byron. The checkbox at the top of the list selects the full page; check the count at the bottom before continuing.
3. Go to **Utilities → Tax Data → Export**.
4. Choose **Return selected**.
5. Check the **Include input data** box at the top. Leave the rest of the options unchecked — the required-field indicator on one of them only applies if you click it.
6. Click **Export**.
7. From the CCH Dashboard, open **Application Links → Batch Manager** (second option under Tax).
8. Refresh the job until the status reads **Ready for download**, then download the file. The filename is blank on download — give it a name your firm will recognize (client name, date, or "Byron import" work fine).

### Send the file to Byron

Hand the downloaded `.dat` to Byron. During onboarding, your CSM receives it directly. Once the self-serve direct integration is live in your account, you'll upload it from the dashboard instead.

Once Byron processes the file, the clients show up in your client list with a **Rolled Forward** status. That's the signal that Byron has last year's context for them.

## Roll forward and the Rolled Forward status

"Roll forward" is the step in your tax software where last year's finalized return becomes this year's starting point — client records, balances, elections, and naming conventions carried into the new tax year as a pro forma return. Byron doesn't do the roll forward itself; that happens upstream in CCH or Lacerte. What Byron does is consume the rolled-forward data and keep its client records aligned with it.

After a CCH bulk import, every populated client shows a **Rolled Forward** status in the Byron client list. That status means Byron has last year's return context for the client and will:

- Route new documents to the right existing record instead of creating a duplicate.
- Carry the CCH client ID through every filing and export, so data lands on the correct return.
- Preserve basis tracking and naming conventions on subsequent filings.

## Incremental imports for CCH

If source documents for a client arrive after you've already started their filing — a late K-1, a corrected 1099, documents for a second submission — you don't need to start over. CCH Axcess firms can send additional documents into an existing filing at any point, and Byron folds them into the workpaper without overwriting what's already there. This matters most during extension season, when K-1s often arrive months after the first set of source documents.

<Note>
  Incremental imports are a CCH Axcess capability. Lacerte firms handle late documents through a fresh iData at export time for the updated filing.
</Note>
