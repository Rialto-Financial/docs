title: "Creating a new client"
description: "Add a new client to Byron during filing creation — set up once, reuse every tax year."
---

In Byron, a **client** is the person (or household) whose return your firm prepares. Each client record holds the minimum information Byron needs to stay in sync with your tax software: a name, a filing status, and a client ID that ties back to the corresponding record in Lacerte or CCH. Create the record once, and the same client is available to every filing you do for them in future tax years.

## Before you create a client

Whether you need to create a client manually depends on how Byron is connected to your tax software.

**If your firm uses CCH Axcess**, your clients are likely already in Byron. During setup, your firm sends a one-time `.dat` export containing every pro forma return from CCH for the current tax year, and Byron pre-populates those clients for you. You'll see them in the client selector with a **Rolled Forward** status. If the client you need is there, pick them — don't create a duplicate.

**If your firm uses Lacerte**, clients are created per filing. Prior-year context — basis, naming conventions, election carryovers — flows into each filing via the iData file you upload at export time, not through a bulk client import.

**If Byron isn't connected to your tax software yet**, start with [Connect your tax software](/getting-started/account-setup#connect-your-tax-software).

## Creating a client

New clients are created as part of starting a new filing. From your dashboard:

<Steps>
  <Step title="From your dashboard, click Add Filing.">
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select:">
    - **Filing type** (e.g., Form 1040)
    - **Tax year**In Client, click Create new client.
  </Step>
  <Step title="In Client, click Create new client.">
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in the client details (fields may vary by filing type):">
    - **Client name**
    - **Email** (optional)
    - **Filing status** — **Single**, **Married Filing Jointly (MFJ)**, or **Married Filing Separately (MFS)**. Head of Household (HOH) and Qualifying Surviving Spouse (QSS) are coming soon.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Create Filing. ">
    Byron opens the filing workspace so you can upload documents right away.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Why Byron asks for so little

Byron intentionally keeps client creation light. Personal details like SSN, date of birth, occupation, phone, and address already live in your tax software — Byron doesn't re-collect them. The client record in Byron exists to route documents, track filings, and point back at the right record in Lacerte or CCH at export time. The identifying details that matter for placement on the return travel with the export, not with the client record.

This has a few practical consequences. Creating a client is fast, even mid-call. You don't risk keeping duplicate copies of sensitive data in two systems. And when you edit a client's personal details later, you do it in your tax software, not Byron.

## Client IDs and your tax software

Every filing exports back to your tax software using the tax-software client ID, so Byron keeps that ID attached to the client record. For CCH Axcess firms, the ID comes from the one-time `.dat` export at setup and pre-fills when you submit a filing. For Lacerte firms, it's handled through the iData flow at export time.

The practical takeaway: when you create a new client, pick the right record — or, for existing clients, select from the list rather than re-creating. A mismatched selection means re-exporting or manually moving data on the tax-software side.

## Reusing a client across years

A client record persists across tax years. When you add a filing for the same client next season, select them from the existing-client toggle and their details carry forward automatically.

For CCH Axcess firms, clients rolled forward in CCH show a **Rolled Forward** status in the client list — Byron has last year's context for basis tracking and naming conventions, and can accept incremental imports if new documents (like late K-1s) arrive after the first pass. For Lacerte firms, that prior-year context flows into each filing via the iData file you upload at export time.

See [Roll forward](/managing-clients/importing-prior-year) for the full picture of how prior-year continuity works across both platforms.
