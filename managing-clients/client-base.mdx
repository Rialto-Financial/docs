This is one of the most important steps in getting Byron set up for your firm — and it does much more than the word "import" suggests. When you connect Byron to your existing tax software, you're building the foundation that every future filing, every uploaded document, and every export will reference.

This page walks through what gets set up, why it matters for every client interaction afterwards, and how to do it for each tax software.

<Note>
  Before you begin, confirm your organization administrator has set up the relevant integrations. See [Setting up your organization on Byron](/getting-started/org-setup) for more.
</Note>

## What this one step actually sets up

When Byron consumes data from your tax software, several things happen at once.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Your full client roster lands in Byron at once" icon="users">
    Every rolled-forward client from your tax software is created as a client record in Byron in a single pass — hundreds or thousands of clients in one go, not one-at-a-time entry.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Each client gets a complete file structure" icon="folder-tree">
    Byron sets up the file structure for every imported client automatically. No manual folder creation, no naming conventions to enforce, no per-client setup — it's all derived from what you already have in your tax software.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Prior-year context comes with each client" icon="layer-group">
    Basis tracking, prior-year balances, election carryovers, naming conventions, and the original client IDs from your tax software are all preserved. That context follows the client through every filing in Byron.
  </Card>

  <Card title="The base is reused for every future upload" icon="link">
    This client base is the foundation Byron uses to route every new document you upload going forward. When a W-2 or K-1 lands in Byron, it's matched to the right existing client and the right tax year automatically — without you having to sort or assign anything.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## What this means for you 

The payoff from this one-time setup compounds across the season.

**Faster processing on every upload.** When clients and IDs are pre-populated, every new document — a client's W-2 in February, a corrected 1099 in March, a late K-1 in August — flows to the right place without anyone manually matching it. Across a season, that's thousands of documents your team doesn't have to sort.

**Consistent organization across your team.** Every client uses the same structure, derived from your tax software rather than from whoever set them up in Byron. Anyone on your team can pick up any client filing and navigate it without needing a custom briefing.

**Cleaner exports back to your tax software.** Because the original tax-software client IDs travel with every export, returns land on the right record in CCH or Lacerte without manual mapping. That cuts out a whole class of "wrong client" errors at export time.

**A foundation that scales with your firm.** As preparers come and go, as you onboard seasonal staff for tax season, as your client list grows year over year, the foundation persists. Each new filing season inherits the structure instead of starting fresh, and new team members work in the same shape of filings as everyone else.

## How to set it up

How you bring your client base into Byron depends on your tax software.

### CCH Axcess — one-time bulk import

CCH Axcess firms do a one-time bulk export of every pro forma return for the current tax year, packaged as a `.dat` file. Byron ingests it, populates every rolled-forward client into your client list with a **Rolled Forward** status, and keeps the original CCH client IDs attached so exports land on the right returns.

#### Export from CCH

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Return Manager and filter to pro forma returns">
    In CCH Axcess Return Manager, filter to this tax year's pro forma returns.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the returns to send">
    The checkbox at the top of the list selects the full page — check the count at the bottom before continuing.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the export utility">
    Go to **Utilities → Tax Data → Export**, then choose **Return selected**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure the export">
    Check the **Include input data** box at the top. Leave the rest of the options unchecked — the required-field indicator on one of them only applies if you click it. Click **Export**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Download the file from Batch Manager">
    From the CCH Dashboard, open **Application Links → Batch Manager** (second option under Tax). Refresh the job until the status reads **Ready for download**, then download the file. The filename is blank on download — rename it to something your firm will recognize (client batch name, date, or "Byron import" works).
  </Step>
</Steps>

#### Send the file to Byron

Hand the downloaded `.dat` to Byron. During onboarding, your CSM receives it directly. Once the self-serve direct integration is live in your account, you'll upload it from the dashboard instead.

Once Byron processes the file, the clients show up in your client list with a **Rolled Forward** status. That's the signal that Byron has last year's context for them.

### Lacerte — per-filing iData

Lacerte firms don't do a single bulk import. Instead, the prior-year context comes through a file called iData, delivered per filing. Each filing you start brings its own iData at export time, which is what gives Byron the basis tracking and naming continuity for that client.

The trade-off: Lacerte firms get per-client context exactly when they need it (at export), but they don't have the full firm-wide client list pre-populated in Byron the way CCH firms do.

## What the Rolled Forward status means

After a CCH bulk import, every populated client shows a **Rolled Forward** status in your client list. That status means Byron has last year's return context for the client and will:

- Route new documents to the right existing record instead of creating a duplicate.
- Carry the CCH client ID through every filing and export, so data lands on the correct return.
- Preserve basis tracking and naming conventions on subsequent filings.

## Known limitations

<Warning>
  **CCH bulk exports can occasionally omit data.** Sometimes a single export may not capture everything in one pass, thus we recommend performing multiple bulk exports to ensure 100% coverage. The bulk export from CCH Axcess sometimes misses a subset of clients or fields without raising an error on the CCH side. This is a CCH-side behavior, not specific to Byron.

  After the initial import, compare your populated Byron client list against your CCH roster and run one or more follow-up exports for anything missing. Your CSM can walk you through this during onboarding.
</Warning>
