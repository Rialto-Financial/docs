## What a SOC 2 audit actually checks

SOC 2 is the AICPA's standard for evaluating how a service organization handles customer data. In practical terms, the audit examined how we:

- Grant, review, and revoke access to systems.
- Encrypt, store, and isolate customer data.
- Monitor for security events and respond to incidents.
- Hire, train, and offboard the people who work on the system.
- Manage the third-party services Byron itself depends on.

## Beyond the audit

Compliance isn't only about the once-a-year examination. Behind the scenes, we run a continuous compliance program that includes:

- **Annual self-assessments** of internal controls to confirm they're still in place and working.
- **Annual risk assessments** covering environmental, regulatory, and technological changes that affect our commitments.
- **Annual penetration testing** by an independent firm.
- **Annual reviews of access permissions** for every user, administrator, and service account.
- **Annual reviews of every critical vendor**, with security and confidentiality terms in writing.
- **Annual testing** of our incident response and business continuity plans.
- **Security awareness training** for every employee within their first month, and refreshers at least once a year.

Findings from these activities feed back into our policies and procedures, which are reviewed at least once a year.
