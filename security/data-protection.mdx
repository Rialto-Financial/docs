Tax data is some of the most sensitive information your firm handles — Social Security numbers, income figures, account numbers, a near-complete picture of each client's financial life. Byron treats it that way. This page walks through what we do to protect it, and what each measure actually means for you.

<Card title="Independent verifications" icon="clipboard-list-check">
  The most important thing on this page isn't anything we say about ourselves — it's what an independent auditor has confirmed. Byron has completed both **SOC 2 Type 1** and **SOC 2 Type 2** examinations — the standard third-party security attestations AICPA-credentialed firms recognize. Type 1 confirmed that our security controls were designed appropriately as of September 2025. Type 2 goes further, confirming that those controls have been operating effectively over a sustained period.

  **What it means for you.** Your firm can satisfy its own due-diligence requirements when adding Byron as a vendor without having to take our word for it. Both reports are available under NDA — email us if you'd like a copy.

  For more, see [Compliance](/security/compliance).
</Card>

## Keeping your data private

Two things have to be true for client data to stay private: it has to be unreadable to anyone who shouldn't see it, and only the right people inside Byron should be able to access it at all.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Data is encrypted everywhere it lives" icon="shield-check">
    Whether it's traveling between your browser and Byron, sitting in our database, or stored in a backup, the data is encrypted using current industry standards.

    If a backup file or a piece of hardware ever ended up somewhere it shouldn't, the contents would be unreadable.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Access is tightly limited inside Byron" icon="block-brick">
    Who has access to your data is under diligent control.

    Only your team touches your data and it is that simple. Our information classification and management policy ensures that client documents are classified as confidential and handled as such.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## When you leave Byron

If your firm decides to stop using Byron, we delete your data and all your accounts within 60 days of your contract ending. Personal information about your clients is deleted or de-identified once it no longer has a legitimate business purpose.
