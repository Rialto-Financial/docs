This page is the big-picture view of how a filing moves through Byron. Each stage links to the page with the full walkthrough, so you can read this end to end first and then dive deeper where it matters.

## Before you start

You'll want:

- Your firm onboarded — see [Setting up your organization](/getting-started/org-setup).
- Your client base in Byron — see [Setting up your client base](/managing-clients/client-base). If your client isn't there yet, you can create one on the fly.
- A document for that client — typically a W-2, 1099, or K-1.

## 1. Open or create a filing

Everything in Byron starts with a filing. From your dashboard, click **Add Filing** to set one up — choosing the filing type, tax year, and client. The filing workspace that opens is where every document, comment, and export for this return lives.

See [Creating clients](/managing-clients/creating-clients) for the full client and filing setup.

## 2. Upload documents

Drag source documents directly onto the filing — one document or a full client package. Byron auto-classifies each file as a W-2, 1099, K-1, or other supported form and routes it to the right place. You don't need to sort, label, or rename anything in advance.

See [Uploading documents](/uploading-documents/uploading-docs) for the full upload flow and [Supported file formats](/uploading-documents/supported-formats) for what Byron accepts today.

## 3. Byron extracts the data

Each uploaded document moves to **Processing** while Byron's agent identifies the form type, pulls every relevant field, runs validation checks, and assigns a confidence score to each value. Most documents take under a minute; denser forms like K-1s with footnotes take several minutes. You can keep working in parallel — Byron processes in the background.

## 4. Review and approve

When a document moves to **Ready for Review**, you open it in a side-by-side view: the source PDF on the left, the extracted fields on the right. Sections Byron is confident in collapse automatically; flagged fields surface for your attention. You verify, fix anything that looks wrong, and approve when ready.

See [The review screen](/reviewing-data/review-screen) and [Review process](/reviewing-data/review-process) for the full review flow.

## 5. Export

Once your filing is fully approved, click **Create an Export** to send it back to your tax software. Byron supports direct paths into Lacerte and CCH Axcess, an Excel-based file for GoSystem, and standalone Excel and collated PDF downloads.

See [Export overview](/exporting/overview) for the full list of destinations and when to use each one.

## What's next

That's the complete Byron workflow — upload, extract, review, approve, export. The detail pages linked above go deeper on each stage when you need them.
