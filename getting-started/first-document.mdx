---
title: "Quick start: your first document"
description: "The best way to understand Byron is to run one real document through it."
---

This guide takes you from upload to export with a single client document. A W-2 or 1099 is usually fastest for a first try, but any supported form works.

**Before you start:**

- One PDF of a tax document for a client
- Your Byron account set up — see [Setting up your account](/getting-started/account-setup) if you haven't yet

<Steps>
  <Step title="Step 1: Add a filing">
    Everything in Byron starts with a filing. From your dashboard, click **Add Filing**. You'll be prompted to:

    - **Filing type** — for example, Form 1040 - Individual
    - **Tax year** — the year this filing is for
    - **Client** — select an existing client, or create a new one by entering their first name, last name, email, and filing status (Single, Married Filing Jointly, or Married Filing Separately)

    Click **Create Filing** to continue. Byron opens the filing workspace — this is where all the documents, exports, and notes for this filing live.

    <Tip>
      If you've already imported prior year data, the client may already exist. Pick them from the existing client list rather than creating a duplicate. See [Importing prior year data](/managing-clients/importing-prior-year).
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Step 2: Upload the document">
    In the filing window, drag and drop your PDF anywhere on the page, or click **Upload** and select the file from your computer.

    You don't need to sort, label, or rename anything first. Byron automatically recognizes whether it's a W-2, 1099, K-1, or other supported form and files it in the right place.

    <Note>
      Byron accepts **PDFs and Excel files (.xlsx)** today. If your source is an image, scan, or Word doc, convert it to PDF first. Zip files aren't supported — extract them and upload the individual files instead.
    </Note>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Step 3: Let Byron extract the data">
    After upload, Byron starts processing immediately. Most documents take under a minute; complex K-1s with footnotes and state schedules may take a bit longer. The document's status changes from **Processing** to **Ready for Review** once it's done.

    While Byron works, it's:

    - Identifying the document type
    - Pulling every relevant field, including footnotes and supplemental schedules on K-1s
    - Running validation checks (e.g. year, recipient, reportable amounts)
    - Scoring how confident it is in each value

    <Tip>
      You don't have to sit and wait. Upload more documents, work on a different filing, or close the tab — Byron keeps processing in the background.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Step 4: Review the extracted data">
    Click the document when it's ready. Byron opens a **side-by-side view**: the original PDF on the left, the extracted fields on the right.

    A few things are designed to save you time:

    - Sections Byron is confident about **collapse automatically** — you only see what needs your attention
    - Fields flagged as **Review** are surfaced at the top of the list
    - Click any field and Byron **anchors** you to the exact spot on the PDF where it pulled that value

    For most W-2s and 1099s, this takes under a minute. For K-1s with many fields, filter by **Review** first to focus on flagged items, then bulk-approve the rest.

    Fix anything that looks wrong by clicking the field and typing a new value. When you're satisfied, click **Approve & Next** on the document.

    See [The review screen](/reviewing-data/review-screen) for the full walkthrough.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Step 5: Export to your tax software">
    Once the document is approved, click **Create an Export** in the top right corner of the filing workspace. Pick your format:

    - **Direct to tax software** — Lacerte, CCH Axcess, or GoSystem
    - **Full Workpaper** (Excel) — a comprehensive workbook consolidating all tax data with structured tabs
    - **Collated PDF** — every document in one file for your working paper

    Byron formats the data to match your platform's import requirements. Open the file in your tax software and the fields populate automatically — no manual re-entry.

    See [Export overview](/exporting/overview) for the full list of export options and when to use each one.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## You've just run a document end-to-end

That's the complete Byron workflow. From here, try it at scale — upload a client's whole package at once, or roll forward a prior year client so the matching happens automatically.
